# Rhode Island Child Care Assistance Program (CCAP) - Working References

- **Official Name**: Starting RIght Child Care Assistance Program (CCAP)

- **Regulation**: 218-RICR-20-00-4

## Eligibility Requirements

### Income Eligibility (Section 4.6)

**Entry Threshold**: Countable income at or below 261% FPL based on family size.
- Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.6.1(A)(1)(a) ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=27))

**Exit/Transitional Threshold**: Eligible families continue receiving benefits until income exceeds 300% FPL.
- Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.6.1(A)(1)(a)(1) ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=27))

**Income Limits by Family Size (2025, from EPI):**

| Family Size | Entry (261% FPL) | Exit (300% FPL) |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | $55,202 | $63,450 |
| 3 | $69,557 | $79,950 |
| 4 | $83,912 | $96,450 |
| 5 | $98,267 | $112,950 |
| 6 | $112,622 | $129,450 |

### Asset Test (Section 4.6.1(B))

**Asset Limit**: $1,000,000 in liquid assets (self-declaration).
- Excludes: primary residence, educational savings accounts, retirement accounts, jointly-held accounts with non-spouse outside household (with documentation).
- Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.6.1(B) ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=28))

### Age Limits (Section 4.3.1(A)(1))

- Minimum: 6 weeks old (was 1 week, updated to 6 weeks in current regs)
- Maximum: Under 13 years
- Exception: Through age 18 with documented disability
- Children turning 13 during certification period remain eligible until age 14 or redetermination
- Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.3.1(A)(1) ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=15))

### Activity Requirements (Section 4.6.2)

- Employment: minimum average 20 hours/week at >= greater of state or federal minimum wage
- Training: minimum 20 hours/week in approved programs
- College: minimum 7 credits at RI public institution (CCRI, RIC, URI) - counts as 21 hours (7 credits x 3 hours)
- Two-parent homes: EACH parent must independently meet 20-hour requirement
- Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.6.2(A) ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=33))

### Citizenship (Section 4.3.1(A)(4))

- Child: must be US citizen or qualified immigrant (no 5-year waiting period)
- Parent/applicant: no citizenship proof required
- Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.3.1(A)(4) ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=16))

### Residency (Section 4.3.1(A)(3))

- Parent and children must be Rhode Island residents
- Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.3.1(A)(3) ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=15))

### Child Support Cooperation (Section 4.3.2)

- Required for families with absent parent(s)
- Good cause exceptions available (domestic violence, etc.)
- Non-cooperation affects only the specific child, not entire family
- Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.3.2 ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=17))

## Financial Unit Definition (Section 4.2 Def #26)

The financial unit includes:
- Dependent children (applicant and non-applicant)
- Parent(s)
- Legal spouse(s) of parent(s)
- All must live in the same household
- May include relatives of acceptable degree as determined by DHS

Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.2 Definition 26 ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=9))

## Income Counting Rules

### Countable Income (Section 4.2 Def #30)

Includes:
- Gross wages, salary, commissions, tips, bonuses, stipends
- Adjusted gross income from self-employment
- Social Security (RSDI)
- Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
- Dividends, interest on savings/bonds
- Income from estates or trusts
- Adjusted gross rental income
- Adjusted gross room and board income
- Public assistance payments
- Unemployment compensation
- Temporary Disability Insurance (TDI)
- Workers' Compensation
- Government/military retirement, private pensions/annuities
- Cash payouts for waiving employer health insurance
- Adoption subsidies
- Alimony
- Child support payments
- Regular contributions from persons not in household
- Royalties, strike benefits
- Trade Readjustment Allowance
- VA Compensation, VA Educational Benefits, spousal/dependent allowances, military allotments
- Americorps payments (NOT Americorps/VISTA)
- Foster care payments (when child IS in assistance unit)
- In-Kind Assistance
- Non-citizen sponsor income

Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.2 Definition 30 ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=10))

### Excluded Income (Section 4.2 Def #23)

Excludes:
1. USDA donated foods value
2. Uniform Relocation Assistance payments (42 USC 4601-4655)
3. Undergraduate educational grants/loans (federal or state)
4. Per capita Indian tribe payments (PL 92-254, 93-134, 94-540)
5. Title VII Nutrition Program for the Elderly benefits
6. Foster grandparent/senior health aide/SCORE/ACE volunteer payments
7. Supplemental food assistance (Child Nutrition Act)
8. Experimental Housing Allowance Program payments
9. Certain Indian tribe receipts (PL 94-114)
10. Alaska Native Claims Settlement Act tax-exempt portions
11. Foster care payments (when child NOT in assistance unit)
12. Food assistance benefits value
13. Government rent/housing subsidies
14. Home energy assistance (state/federal/nonprofit)
15. College work study income
16. Earned income of dependent child in financial unit
17. WIOA stipends, earned income, reimbursements
18. EITC refunds and advance payments
19. Loans/grants used for non-living costs (scholarships)
20. Social Security PASS/IRWE program monies
21. Income of parents with whom teen parent resides
22. Section 8 Utility Payment
23. Veterans Aid and Attendant Allowances
24. Americorps/VISTA volunteer payments
25. Rhode Island Works (RIW) cash assistance
26. Veterans Disability Pension (service-connected disability)

Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.2 Definition 23 ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=6))

### Income Conversion

- Weekly income converted to monthly using 4.3333 weeks/month factor
- Prospective budgeting used (reasonable expectation of future income)
- Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.6.1(A)(1)(c) ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=28))

## Co-Payment / Family Cost Sharing (Section 4.6.1(C))

### Co-Payment Rate Table

| Level | FPL Range | Family Share |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | <= 100% FPL | No family share ($0) |
| 1 | >100% to 125% FPL | 2% of countable gross income |
| 2 | >125% to 150% FPL | 5% of countable gross income |
| 3 | >150% to 261% FPL | 7% of countable gross income |
| 4 (Transitional) | >261% to 300% FPL | 7% of countable gross income |

Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.6.1(C)(1)(c) ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=30))

### Co-Payment Rules
- Only ONE co-payment per family, regardless of number of children
- Co-payment assigned to youngest eligible child enrolled (highest rate child)
- Distributed among providers only when total exceeds youngest child's rate
- RI Works recipients: $0 co-payment
- Housing insecure families: $0 co-payment
- Childcare educator pilot participants: $0 co-payment (through 7/31/2028)
- Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.6.1(C)(2) ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=31))

## Benefit Calculation

The CCAP benefit is calculated as:
```
Benefit = CCAP Maximum Weekly Rate - Family Share (co-payment)
```

Where:
- **CCAP Maximum Weekly Rate** depends on: provider type (center/family/exempt), QRIS star rating (1-5), child's age category (infant/toddler/preschool/school age), and authorized time tier (FT/3QT/HT/QT)
- **Family Share** = percentage of gross countable income based on FPL level (0%, 2%, 5%, or 7%)
- Family share is a weekly amount applied to the youngest child's provider rate
- If family share exceeds youngest child's rate, remainder distributed to other providers

**Time Tiers (Section 4.12(B)(1)):**
- Full Time: 30+ hours/week
- Three-Quarter Time: 20-29 hours/week
- Half Time: 10-19 hours/week
- Quarter Time: 0-9 hours/week

**Age Categories (Section 4.12(B)(2)):**
- Infant: 6 weeks to 18 months (centers split infant/toddler)
- Toddler: 18 months to 3 years
- Preschool: 3 years to 1st grade entry (includes all kindergarten)
- School Age: 1st grade to 13 years

Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.12 ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=63))

## Certification Period

- Minimum 24 months (recently updated from 12 months)
- Redetermination before end of certification period
- Renewal form sent no later than 1st day of final month
- Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.4.4 ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=24))

## Special Programs

### RI Works CCAP (Section 4.5)
- RI Works (TANF) cash assistance recipients eligible for CCAP
- $0 co-payment as supportive service
- Must have approved employment plan
- Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.5 ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=25))

### CCAP for Child Care Educators/Staff Pilot (218-RICR-20-00-13)
- **URL**: https://rules.sos.ri.gov/regulations/Part/218-20-00-13
- Income eligibility up to 300% FPL
- Must work 20+ hours/week at DHS-licensed child care program
- $0 co-payment
- Extended through 7/31/2028
- Source: https://dhs.ri.gov/programs-and-services/child-care/child-care-providers/CCAP-for-Child-Care-Staff

### Temporary Job Search (Section 4.6.4(A)(4))
- Up to 3 months of CCAP during active job search
- Limited to half/three-quarter time authorization
- Maximum 2 lifetime occurrences per household
- Source: 218-RICR-20-00-4 Section 4.6.4(A)(4) ([PDF](https://dhs.ri.gov/media/9236/download?language=en#page=37))

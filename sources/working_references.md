### SSA POMS - Indiana State Supplementary Payments
- **URL**: https://secure.ssa.gov/poms.nsf/lnx/0501401001CHI
- **Title**: SI CHI01401.001 - (IN) Indiana State Supplementary Payments (RTN 400 - 02/2007)
- **Effective**: October 5, 2022 to Present
- **Key facts**:
  - No mandatory supplementary payment recipients remain in Indiana
  - Two categories of optional supplementary payments:
    1. **Room and Board Assistance (RBA)** for SSI/Medicaid recipients in residential care facilities
    2. **Medicaid-certified facility supplement** ($22/month to bring personal needs allowance to $52)
  - RBA maximum provider expense: $39.35/day ($1,196.90/month) as of January 1, 2004
  - Personal needs allowance: $52.00/month (effective July 2002)
  - RBA payment is "totally excludable for calculating the SSI payment amount"
  - SSI recipients in Medicaid facilities receive $30 SSI + $22 state supplement = $52 personal needs
  - The $22 supplement is excluded from SSI income calculations
- **Access**: Fetched successfully

### Indiana FSSA Medicaid Policy Manual Chapter 5000 - SAPN
- **URL**: https://www.in.gov/fssa/ompp/files/Medicaid_PM_5000.pdf
- **Title**: Indiana Health Coverage Program Policy Manual Chapter 5000 - Supplemental Assistance for Personal Needs
- **Key facts**:
  - Official program name: **Supplemental Assistance for Personal Needs (SAPN)**
  - Established by Indiana PL 294-2001, effective July 1, 2002
  - Eligible individuals in health care facilities receive up to $22/month
  - **Eligibility**: Must be (1) receiving Medicaid, (2) residing in a Medicaid-certified health care facility throughout the calendar month, and (3) receiving a $30 reduced SSI benefit
  - **Benefit calculation**: $52 Medicaid Personal Needs Allowance minus budgeted earned and unearned income = SAPN amount ($1 to $22)
  - SAPN payments are NOT countable income in Medicaid determination
  - Legal citations: IC 12-15-32-6.5, 405 IAC 7-1-1
  - Payments issued as warrants (checks) — direct deposit terminated January 2011
- **Access**: Fetched successfully (PDF extracted via pdftotext)

#### IC 12-10-6-1 — Eligibility of county home residents; amount; rate; personal allowance

Sec. 1. (a) An individual who:
(1) is at least sixty-five (65) years of age, is blind, or has a disability; and
(2) is a resident of a county home;
is eligible to receive assistance payments from the state if the individual would be eligible for assistance under the Supplemental Security Income program except for the fact that the individual is residing in a county home.

(b) The recipient shall be paid or allowed to retain from the recipient's income a monthly personal allowance that:
(1) is fifty-two dollars ($52);
(2) is exempt from income eligibility consideration by the division; and
(3) may be exclusively used by the recipient for personal needs.

(c) In addition to the amount that may be retained as a personal allowance under this section, an individual may retain an amount equal to the individual's state and local income tax liability. The amount that may be retained during a month may not exceed one-third (1/3) of the individual's state and local income tax liability for the calendar quarter in which the month occurs. The amount is exempt from income eligibility consideration by the division. The amount retained shall be used by the individual to pay state or local income taxes owed.

(d) In addition to the amounts that may be retained under subsections (b) and (c), an eligible individual may retain an eligible settlement payment. The payment is exempt from income eligibility consideration by the division.

(e) The personal allowance for one (1) month for an individual described in subsection (a) is the amount the individual is entitled to retain under subsection (b) plus an amount equal to one-half (1/2) of the remainder of:
(1) gross earned income for that month; minus
(2) the sum of:
(A) sixteen dollars ($16); plus
(B) the amount withheld from the person's paycheck for that month for payment of state income taxes and taxes prescribed by the federal Insurance Contribution Act (26 U.S.C. 3101 et seq.); plus
(C) transportation expenses for that month; plus
(D) any mandatory expenses required by the employer as a condition of employment.

#### IC 12-10-6-2.1 — Residential care assistance; eligibility; alternative placement for individuals with an intellectual disability; personal allowance and other amounts retained

Sec. 2.1. (a) An individual who:
(1) is at least eighteen (18) years of age, is blind, or has a disability;
(2) is incapable of residing in the individual's own home because of dementia, mental illness, or a physical disability;
(3) requires a degree of care less than that provided by a health care facility licensed under IC 16-28;
(4) can be adequately cared for in a residential care setting; and
(5) has not made any asset transfer prohibited under the state plan or in 42 U.S.C. 1396p(c) in order to qualify for assistance;
is eligible for residential care assistance.

(b) Individuals with an intellectual disability may not be admitted to a home or facility that provides residential care under this chapter.

(c) A service coordinator employed by the division may:
(1) evaluate a person seeking admission to a home or facility under subsection (a); or
(2) evaluate a person who has been admitted to a home or facility under subsection (a), including a review of the person's record at the home or facility.
If the service coordinator determines the person evaluated under this subsection has an intellectual disability, the service coordinator may recommend an alternative placement for the person.

(d) Except as provided in section 5 of this chapter, residential care consists of only room, board, and laundry, with minimal administrative direction. The recipient may retain from the recipient's income a monthly personal allowance of fifty-two dollars ($52). The amount is exempt from income eligibility consideration by the division and may be exclusively used by the recipient for personal needs. However, if the recipient's income is less than the amount of the personal allowance, the division shall pay the difference between the amount of the personal allowance and the recipient's income. A reserve or an accumulation of the personal allowance, together with other sources, may not be allowed to exceed the state's resource allowance allowed for adult recipients of supplemental assistance or Medicaid as established by the rules of the office of Medicaid policy and planning.

(e) In addition to the amount that may be retained as a personal allowance under this section, an individual may retain an amount equal to the individual's state and local income tax liability. The amount that may be retained during a month may not exceed one-third (1/3) of the individual's state and local income tax liability for the calendar quarter in which that month occurs. The amount is exempt from income eligibility consideration by the division. The amount retained shall be used by the individual to pay state or local income taxes owed.

(f) In addition to the amounts that may be retained under subsections (d) and (e), an eligible individual may retain an eligible settlement payment. The payment is exempt from income eligibility consideration by the division.

(g) The personal allowance for one (1) month for an individual described in subsection (a) is the amount the individual is entitled to retain under subsection (d) plus an amount equal to one-half (1/2) of the remainder of:
(1) gross earned income for that month; minus
(2) the sum of:
(A) sixteen dollars ($16); plus
(B) the amount withheld from the person's paycheck for that month for payment of state income taxes and taxes prescribed by the federal Insurance Contribution Act (26 U.S.C. 3101 et seq.); plus
(C) transportation expenses for that month; plus
(D) any mandatory expenses required by the employer as a condition of employment.

(h) An individual who, before September 1, 1983, has been admitted to a home or facility that provides residential care under this chapter is eligible for residential care in the home or facility.

(i) The director of the division may contract with the division of mental health and addiction or the division of disability and rehabilitative services to purchase services for individuals with a mental illness or a developmental disability by providing the contracted agency with funds from the appropriation for community based residential care programs established under IC 12-22-2 or community residential facilities established under IC 12-11-1.1-1.

(j) A person with a mental illness may not be placed in a Christian Science facility listed and certified by the Commission for Accreditation of Christian Science Nursing Organizations/Facilities, Inc., unless the facility is licensed under IC 16-28.

As added by P.L.1-2003, SEC.52. Amended by P.L.141-2006, SEC.42; P.L.99-2007, SEC.61; P.L.121-2008, SEC.3; P.L.146-2008, SEC.228; P.L.229-2011, SEC.119; P.L.6-2012, SEC.89; P.L.117-2015, SEC.18.

#### IC 12-10-6-5 — Individuals with a mental illness; comprehensive care plan; rules

Sec. 5. (a) A home or facility that provides residential care under this chapter to an individual with a mental illness must provide a comprehensive care plan.

(b) The comprehensive care plan required by this section must include the following:
(1) Proper medication management, including administration, monitoring, and side effect identification.
(2) Social services that include the following:
(A) Appropriate socialization opportunities.
(B) Personal maintenance skills programs.
(C) Educational, training, occupational, and work programs.
(D) Opportunities for progression into less restrictive and more independent living arrangements.
(3) Appropriate alternate placement if the individual's needs cannot be met by the facility.

(d) The Indiana department of health shall, in coordination with the division of mental health and addiction, adopt rules under IC 4-22-2 to govern:
(1) residential care; and
(2) the comprehensive care plan;
provided to individuals with a mental illness who reside under this chapter in a home or facility that provides residential care.

## SSA 2011 Indiana State Supplementary Payment Report (User-Downloaded)

Source: https://www.ssa.gov/policy/docs/progdesc/ssi_st_asst/2011/in.html (converted to PDF by user)
PDF screenshots at 600 DPI: /tmp/in-ssp-user-doc-1-page-1.png, /tmp/in-ssp-user-doc-1-page-2.png

### Program Overview
- **Administration**: Family and Social Services Administration (FSSA), Division of Aging
- **Effective date**: July 1, 1976
- **Statutory basis**: Indiana Public Law 46, Acts of 1976
- **Funding**: 100% state funds (both administration and assistance)
- **Passalong method**: Maintaining payment levels
- **Place of application**: FSSA county offices of the Division of Family Resources
- **Total expenditures**: $8,453,205.54 for calendar year 2010

### Scope of Coverage
Optional state supplement provided to all adult Medicaid or SSI recipients who, because of age, blindness, or disability, are unable to reside in their own home and need care in a residential facility. **Children under age 18 are not eligible** for optional supplementation.

### Resource Limitations
- Individual: $1,500 nonexempt real and personal property
- Couple: $2,250 (if spouse resides in same facility, resources of both counted)
- Life insurance cash surrender value disregarded if face value ≤ $10,000 and beneficiary is funeral director or person's estate
- $10,000 limitation reduced by irrevocable burial trust or prepaid funeral amounts
- Real property offered for sale or rent is exempt

### Income Exclusions
- $16 sheltered workshop employment incentive
- Mandatory earnings deductions
- One-half of remaining earnings

### Payment Calculation Method
A standard applies that is established by the state for the total SSI payment. The federal SSI payment and any countable income are deducted from the state standard. The remainder is the state supplementation.

### Table 1: Payment Levels (January 2011, in dollars)

| Living Arrangement | Combined Fed+State (Individual) | Combined Fed+State (Couple) | State Supplement (Individual) | State Supplement (Couple) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Licensed residential facility | $1,501.06 | (treated as individuals) | $827.06 | (treated as individuals) |
| Medicaid facility | $52.00 | $104.00 | $22.00 | $44.00 |

**Notes:**
- Licensed residential facility: Combined benefit up to $1,501.06 (state portion up to $827.06), including personal allowance of $52/month. Lesser amounts paid depending on cost of facility and income.
- Couples in these arrangements are considered as individuals one month after leaving an independent living arrangement.
- Medicaid facility: Personal needs allowance of $52 ($22 state supplement + $30 federal SSI reduced benefit)

### Table 2: Recipients (January 2011)

| Living Arrangement | Total | Aged | Blind | Disabled |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| All recipients | 3,442 | 797 | 6 | 2,639 |
| Licensed residential facility | 1,517 | 322 | 1 | 1,194 |
| Medicaid facility | 1,925 | 475 | 5 | 1,445 |

### Definitions
- **Licensed residential facility**: Room, board, and laundry with minimal administrative direction. Must be licensed by Indiana Department of Health and approved for Room and Board Assistance program by FSSA. Can be publicly or privately owned, for-profit or not-for-profit.
- **Medicaid facility**: Recipients residing in a federal Code D living arrangement (Medicaid-certified nursing homes/institutional care).

### Financial Responsibility
- Spouse for spouse when residing in the same facility
- No recoveries, liens, or assignments

### State Assistance for Special Needs
State does not provide assistance for special needs.
